Cornwall Butterfly Conservation welcomes Georgina Paul to the role of Volunteer Coordinator.

Georgina, who likes to be called Georgie, was selected from a strong field in early April. The role is the only paid one in the branch and is funded from money raised by the branch.

Georgie will work for CBC 30 days in the year, and will fit this role around her many other commitments. As CBC Volunteer Coordinator, she will organise, lead and manage field work, which will include surveys of some of our rarer butterfly species, habitat management, public engagement and fund-raising. As the title of her job implies, her work will revolve around and depend upon CBC’s amazing band of volunteers, who are ready and raring to go after the last two years’ Covid restrictions.

After graduating with a degree in zoological conservation in Cornwall, Georgie has applied her training and developed her skills in Thailand, Belize, Ecuador and India, working variously with manatees and elephants, and in forest ecology, working with local communities, most recently in a leadership role.

Brimming with excitement about her new job with Cornwall Butterfly Conservation, Georgie said, “I have dedicated my life to protecting the environment in every way that I can and now I can help to conserve Cornwall’s Lepidoptera. I know that it will be a steep learning curve for me, but with the support of all those with so much experience in the branch, I will hit the ground running. I can’t wait to start - it’s a dream come true!”

Tristram Besterman, Chair of CBC, warmly welcomes the new Volunteer Coordinator to the Cornwall Branch. Her enthusiasm is infectious. “Georgie brings new energy to our work. After the difficulties and set-backs of the last two years, it is wonderful to have Georgie in post to revitalise our volunteer programmes, which lie at the heart of our conservation work. I know that our volunteers are champing at the bit, and they will soon be hearing from Georgie. I’m sure that Georgie will build on all the successes of her predecessor, Jo Poland, and enable CBC’s volunteers to resume their all-important work.”

The CBC Conservation Working Group has developed a Butterfly Conservation Plan for 2022-23 and this will guide Georgie Paul’s work priorities.
