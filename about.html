What we do

We conserve butterflies and moths in Cornwall. The short video, Cornwall’s Butterflies Back from the Brink summarises the work of the branch.
Create, co-ordinate and analyse
We create, co-ordinate and analyse thousands of records of butterflies and moths seen in the county, submitted every year by an army of volunteer nature-lovers. This data enables us to track what is happening to species over time across the County. This helps us to understand their ecology better and to target our conservation work more effectively.
Manage and improve habitats
We manage and improve habitats of our nationally rarest and most endangered butterfly species, to ensure their populations can flourish in Cornwall.
Carry out surveys
We carry out surveys targeted on our most vulnerable butterfly species, to assess populations the impact of our work.
Provide a programme of public field trips
We provide a programme of public field trips to key locations to see butterflies and moths from April to September every year.
Raise public awareness
We raise public awareness of butterfly and moth conservation, through social media, talks and our presence at events throughout the county.
