Cornwall Butterfly Conservation has a new website

We are launching our new website, designed to make information about butterflies and moths in Cornwall more accessible, to help you to find out what Cornwall Butterfly Conservation is doing and, most important of all, to make it easier for you to join in.

An effective online presence for any organisation is not optional: it is an indispensable means of communication in today’s digital world. Creating a new website would normally be an expensive undertaking, diverting hard-won funds away from our core purpose of conserving butterflies and moths.

But help was at hand: local Cornish business, StoryX rode to our rescue! StoryX builds world class websites for purpose-driven businesses from start-ups to corporates like the NHS. Alongside this commercial work, The StoryX Foundation builds websites free of charge for small charities, community groups and good causes, without ever compromising on quality.

“Cornwall Butterfly Conservation is greatly indebted to StoryX, a creative enterprise based in Cornwall, which worked with us to develop this website. Through the values of its founder, StoryX has the natural environment at its heart, and generously provides its services free of charge to Cornwall Butterfly Conservation. The website is hosted by Eco Hosting, a green, UK-based, environmental platform.”
Tristram Besterman, Chair, Cornwall Butterfly Conservation

“People often ask what the X stands for in StoryX … It is meant to remind people that the ending to our story is not yet written. We believe that we change the ending of any story from our personal story to the story of our planet. Cornwall Butterfly Conservation was the first website built by the StoryX Foundation, it was a pleasure working together and we hope that the website can be a part of creating a positive future for the organisation and the vital work they do."
James Murphy, Founder, StoryX

We hope you like our new website, which is designed to be attractive and easy to navigate. We want it to provide a positive experience for every user and one that is engineered to work equally well on all devices. Nothing is ever perfect, so we welcome feedback and suggestions for improvement.
