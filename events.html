CORNWALL BUTTERFLY CONSERVATION FIELD TRIPS AND EVENTS 2023

Welcome to Cornwall Butterfly Conservation’s programme of field trips for 2023; an exciting mix of new sites and old favourites. As before, we have also organised a few trips in partnership with other local conservation organisations. Increasingly, the importance of recording is recognised so we will be encouraging everyone to note what they find on these field trips so that we can continue to build a better picture of lepidoptera distribution across Cornwall.

Our field trips are fun and friendly and open to all - including accompanied children - whatever level of experience you have and whether or not you’re a member of Cornwall Butterfly Conservation. Please always come prepared for Cornwall’s changeable weather and mixed terrain – waterproofs, good walking shoes or boots, sun cream etc., as well as water and lunch/snacks.

Please be aware that we would prefer dogs not to be brought because we are in wildlife sensitive areas.

Postcodes have been included. However, please bear in mind that they may not be as accurate as grid references in pinpointing meeting places, particularly on the coastal walks where there are no obvious landmarks and the postcode covers a larger than usual area so you may find the following link useful - grab a grid

  • Saturday 13th May: FELLOVER BRAKE

Near St. Breward 12 noon

A meeting hopefully to see one of the UK’s most declining and endangered butterflies, the Pearl-Bordered Fritillary, at this hillside known to be reliable for them. Directions: from the “Old Inn” St Breward go north and down the hill to the junction. Turn left, continue down and the road levels out before a bridge over the Camel. We park and meet along this road. If coming north on the B3266 go past the signs for St Breward and St Tudy and watch out for a right-hand bend and then a left soon after. At this bend take the right-hand minor road signposted St Breward and the road drops down the hill to the bridge and the meeting point. (SX088778) Postcode PL30 4PQ. Please bring a packed lunch.

Our new website features a modern design, easy navigation, and up-to-date information about Cornwall Butterfly Conservation. We believe that this new website will provide a more engaging and informative experience for our members and the general public, helping to promote the work that we do.

Leader and contact: Alan Stapleton (01208 850085)
