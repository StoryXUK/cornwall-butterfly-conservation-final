Some good places to see butterflies in Cornwall

Here are some places where you can see some of Cornwall’s rarer butterflies. These have been selected because they are accessible using public footpaths, some with ‘open access’.

The grid reference and link to Google maps will help you to find the entrance to the site. The relevant 1:25,000 Ordnance Survey map is highly recommended.

Details of the terrain, land ownership and descriptions of each site can be found in Chapter 7 of Butterflies of Cornwall: Atlas for the Twenty-first Century, pages 222-244 (insert LINK). The importance of preparing for your visit cannot be overstated, in relation to assessing the suitability of the terrain, clothing, footwear and provisions. Please observe the Countryside Code.

Butterflies of Cornwall will also enable you to plan your visit when the species of butterfly can be expected to be flying. CBC organises a programme of escorted visits to many of these sites with an expert at the right time of the year (insert LINK).

We hope you enjoy a productive and safe visit.
